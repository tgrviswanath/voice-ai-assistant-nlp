--- ENTRY 1: Machine Learning ---
Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence that enables systems
to learn from data without being explicitly programmed. Key types include
supervised learning (labelled data), unsupervised learning (pattern discovery),
and reinforcement learning (reward-based). Popular frameworks include
TensorFlow, PyTorch, and scikit-learn.

--- ENTRY 2: Climate Change ---
Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather
patterns. Since the industrial revolution, human activities — primarily burning
fossil fuels — have been the main driver. Consequences include rising sea levels,
more frequent extreme weather events, and ecosystem disruption. The Paris
Agreement aims to limit warming to 1.5-2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.

--- ENTRY 3: How Vaccines Work ---
Vaccines work by training the immune system to recognise and fight specific
pathogens. They introduce a weakened or inactivated form of a virus or bacteria,
or a piece of it (like a protein), which triggers an immune response. The body
then creates memory cells so it can respond quickly if exposed to the real pathogen.
mRNA vaccines, like those for COVID-19, instruct cells to produce a protein that
triggers this immune response.

--- ENTRY 4: The Internet ---
The internet is a global network of interconnected computers that communicate
using standardised protocols (TCP/IP). Data is broken into packets, routed
through multiple nodes, and reassembled at the destination. The World Wide Web
is a service built on top of the internet using HTTP/HTTPS protocols. DNS
(Domain Name System) translates human-readable domain names into IP addresses.
